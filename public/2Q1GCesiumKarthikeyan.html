Leni's Political Career

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Leni Robredo was the second female vice president after Gloria Macapagal Arroyo and the first vice president from Bicol. In 2016, she was rewarded by the government of Thailand for her contributions in women's empowerment and gender equality. Under her leadership, the Office of the Vice President received the ISO 9001:2015 certification for the office's quality management systems. She served as the representative of Camarines Sur's 3rd district from 2013 until her inauguration as vice president in 2016. In the election, she defeated Bongbong Marcos by 278,566 votes.

From 1988 to 2008, Robredo became the coordinator of Sentro ng Alternatibong Lingap Panligan or simply SALIGAN, a Naga-based alternative legal support group. In addition, Robredo founded the Lakas ng Kababaihan ng Naga Federation, an organization that provided training and livelihood opportunities for women in 1989. In 2012, Robredo was named chairperson of the Liberal Party in Camarines Sur. She then ran in Camarines Sur's 3rd congressional district during the Philippine general elections of 2013.
